ExpenseKing Help Center

Everything you need to get the most out of your app.

👋 Getting Started

1

Add a wallet — Go to the Wallets tab and tap +. Give it a name (e.g. Cash, GCash, Bank) and set your starting balance.

2

Add categories — Go to the Categories tab and tap +. Pick an icon and color that makes it easy to identify.

3

Log your first expense — Tap the + button on the Expenses screen. Enter the amount, pick a category and wallet, and save.

Tip: You're taken straight to the category profile after creating a category — that's a good time to set a budget right away.

👛 Wallets

How do I add money to a wallet?

Open the wallet, then tap Deposit / Adjust. You can add funds, record a withdrawal, or correct the balance.

Why does my balance change when I log an expense?

Every expense is deducted from the wallet you selected. Your wallet balance always reflects your actual available funds.

What balance is shown in the header?

The header shows your main wallet's balance. You can set a wallet as your main one from its profile. Tap the header to open that wallet directly.

Can I have multiple wallets?

Yes — add as many as you need. The Wallets tab shows each wallet's balance and the total across all of them.

🗂️ Categories & Budgets

How do I set a budget for a category?

Open the category profile and tap Set Budget. Choose a period (Daily, Weekly, or Monthly), enter your limit, and save.

What's the difference between Pace tracking and Total tracking?

Pace — tracks your daily spending rate against an expected pace. Best for categories you spend in regularly (food, transport).

Total — just tracks if your period total stays within budget. Best for monthly bills or one-time payments.

What does the orange marker on the budget bar mean?

It shows where your spending should be right now based on how far through the period you are. If your bar is to the left of the marker, you're underspending. To the right — you're ahead of pace.

What happens to expenses if I delete a category?

They're moved to Uncategorized — no spending history is lost.

📈 Spending Momentum

What is Spending Momentum?

It's a quick signal that tells you whether you're spending more or less than you normally do in a category — without you having to do any math. Think of it as your personal spending pulse.

What do the messages mean?

First spending today — you've just logged your first expense in this category today.

About the same as usual — today's spending is in line with your normal pattern. Nothing to worry about.

X% lower than usual — you're spending less than usual today. A good sign if you're trying to cut back.

X% higher than usual — you're spending more than usual today. Worth keeping an eye on.

Why does it say "No clear pattern yet"?

You're still building history in this category. Keep logging and momentum will kick in soon.

Does it show for budgeted categories?

No — if a category has a budget set, the budget progress bar takes over. It gives you more specific information than momentum alone.

🔔 Spending Reminders

How do I set up daily reminders?

Go to Settings → Spending Reminders, toggle reminders on, then tap Add Reminder Time to pick your preferred time.

The notification shows up but doesn't pop on screen.

Go to Settings → Apps → ExpenseKing → Notifications → Spending Reminders and make sure Pop on screen is enabled.

What do the notification buttons do?

Add Expense opens the add expense form directly. Remind Later re-schedules a reminder 30 minutes from now.

💡 Insights

What does the Insights screen show?

A daily spending chart for the selected period, your top spending categories, and overall trends.

Can I see spending for a specific day?

Yes — tap any dot on the chart and the Top Categories list updates to show only that day's breakdown.

Tapping a category in Insights — where does it go?

It opens that category's full profile with its chart, stats, and budget details.

❓ Common Questions

Is my data stored online?

Your expense data is stored locally on your device only. No account, no cloud, no personal financial data ever leaves your phone. The app does display ads via Google AdMob, which may collect device and usage information as described in our Privacy Policy.

What happens if I uninstall the app?

Your data will be lost since it's stored locally. We recommend taking a screenshot of your key stats before uninstalling.

How do I change the app theme?

Go to Settings and toggle between Light, Dark, or System default.
